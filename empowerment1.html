IBOM-LED EMPOWERS 800 ENTREPRENEURS WITH N500,000 START-UP FUNDS

Since taking office in May 2023, Governor Umo Eno of Akwa Ibom State has prioritized supporting indigenous entrepreneurs to foster economic growth and self-reliance. Through his leadership, the Ibom Leadership and Entrepreneurial Centre (Ibom-LED) has become a cornerstone of entrepreneurial development, inspiring and equipping Akwa Ibom residents with the tools needed to succeed in business.

Empowering Entrepreneurs Through Ibom-LED

Governor Umo Eno established Ibom-LED to address the need for sustainable economic development and reduce dependence on political patronage. By repurposing the previously underutilized E-Library built by a former administration, the center has been transformed into a hub of innovation and business education. Under the leadership of Imo-abasi Jacob, Managing Director of AKICORP, Ibom-LED is equipping entrepreneurs with the knowledge and skills necessary to modernize and grow their businesses.

Comprehensive Training Programs

Ibom-LED offers a variety of training programs tailored to nurture entrepreneurial talent:

• Entrepreneurship Accelerator Programme (EAP)
• Business Mastery Program (BMP)
• Business Leadership and Entrepreneurship Program (BLEP)
These initiatives focus on critical areas such as operations, communication, marketing, negotiation, and leadership, empowering participants with practical business skills.
Additionally, the Transforming Nigerian Youths Program (TNY) provides essential personal and professional development skills, while the Ibom Ignite Business Grant Award Program (IIBGA) and the Start-Up Accelerator & Incubation Programme (SAIP) offer funding and mentorship to early-stage startups.

Strategic Investments in Youth Development

Governor Eno’s administration kickstarted the Ibom-LED initiative with a ₦250 million investment, enabling the training of 400 participants in the first batch. Graduates of the program receive ₦500,000 each in business support grants, reinforcing the administration’s commitment to self-reliance and economic empowerment. The initiative has already supported 800 trainees, providing them with grants and branding assistance for their businesses. Among the success stories is Jones Okon Akpan, a person living with a disability, who demonstrated exceptional entrepreneurial spirit by gifting the governor a pair of shoes he crafted. Governor Eno, moved by this act, doubled Jones’ grant and pledged to support 5,000 entrepreneurs like him, emphasizing the administration’s dedication to inclusivity.

Transformative Impact on Akwa Ibom’s Economy

The Ibom-LED initiative exemplifies Governor Umo Eno’s vision of transforming Akwa Ibom into a hub of entrepreneurial excellence. By investing in training, mentorship, and financial support, the program is unlocking the potential of the state’s youth and fostering sustainable development. Governor Eno’s strategic approach to empowering entrepreneurs not only addresses immediate economic challenges but also lays the groundwork for long-term growth and prosperity in Akwa Ibom State. Through Ibom-LED, the administration is turning the state into a beacon of innovation and self-reliance, ensuring a brighter future for its citizens.

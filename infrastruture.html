HOW UMO ENO’S LEADERSHIP IS DRIVING AKWA IBOM’S INFRASTRUCTURE TRANSFORMATION

Under the dynamic leadership of Governor Umo Eno, Akwa Ibom State is experiencing significant infrastructural transformation, laying the groundwork for sustainable growth and development across the state. Speaking during a press briefing in Uyo, the Honourable Commissioner for Works and Fire Service, Professor Eno Ibanga, provided updates on the state’s ongoing road and infrastructure projects. He highlighted the administration's efforts since its inauguration on May 29, 2023, emphasizing its dual focus on completing inherited projects and initiating new ones under the ARISE Agenda.

Professor Ibanga revealed that Governor Umo Eno has allocated N47.93 billion to 36 projects inherited from previous administrations, alongside N4.04 billion for 51 emergency intervention projects, totaling N51.96 billion. These investments reflect the administration’s commitment to honoring past promises while building a foundation for future progress. In addition to completing inherited projects, the ARISE Agenda has launched 61 transformative projects, with several already completed and commissioned. Notable projects include the Akpan Andem Market, the Amphitheatre Christmas Village Arena, the Atiku Abubakar underground tunnel, and the Goretti Secondary School Road. A total of N151.66 billion has been released for 50 ARISE Agenda projects, with an additional N4.54 billion allocated for 11 emergency interventions. This brings the total investment in infrastructure under the ARISE Agenda to an impressive N156.20 billion.

As of October 18, 2024, the combined expenditure on inherited and new projects has reached N208.16 billion, underscoring Governor Eno’s focus on infrastructural development as a driver of economic growth and improved living standards. Professor Ibanga also shared updates on key road projects, including the much-anticipated Tabernacle Road project, which faced weather-related delays. In response to commuter needs, the administration has awarded contracts for the construction of Uyo Village Road and Ikpa Road up to Udoette Street, further enhancing mobility and safety for residents.

Several major roads are nearing completion and will soon be inaugurated, including:
• The 6.97 km Ukpana-Akpabom-Ikwe Road with a 30-meter span bridge in ONNA and Mkpat Enin LGAs.
• 2.45 km internal roads at Block G, Anua Ifa Ikot Okpon Housing Estate, Uyo.
• 10.519 km Mkpat Enin-Ikot Ubo-Ikot Ntot-Nkikara Road in Mkpat Enin/Ikot Abasi LGAs.
• 22.75 km Ikot Ekaide-Ikot Okoro-Ibritam Road with two bridges in Oruk Anam LGA.

To address intra-city road maintenance, the administration has engaged two additional contractors, alongside AKROIMA, to begin remedial works once the rainy season ends. Residents were urged to avoid obstructing drainage systems and making unauthorized road modifications to ensure the durability of the infrastructure. Professor Ibanga reiterated Governor Umo Eno’s commitment to completing all projects, both inherited and initiated. He assured that the Ministry of Works and Fire Service would press contractors to adhere to timelines as the dry season begins.

Under the ARISE Agenda, Akwa Ibom is entering an era of unparalleled infrastructural growth, setting the stage for long-term economic development and an enhanced quality of life for its citizens. Governor Umo Eno’s administration continues to drive progress with vision, transparency, and tangible results, moving Akwa Ibom State forward, one project at a time.

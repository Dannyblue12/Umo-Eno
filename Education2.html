Transforming Education in Akwa Ibom: Governor Umoh Eno’s Vision for a Brighter Future

Governor Umoh Eno is spearheading a series of impactful reforms to reshape education in Akwa Ibom State, aligning with the ARISE Agenda—a strategic framework that emphasizes development in Agriculture, Rural improvement, Infrastructure, Security, and Education. His administration’s efforts target creating a conducive learning environment, fostering skill development, and ensuring free access to quality education at primary and secondary levels.

Building Model Schools and Developing Skills

Governor Eno is committed to ensuring that every child in the state benefits from modern educational facilities. The commissioning of the Christ the King Model Primary School and Government School Ewet Offot marks the beginning of a broader vision to establish model schools in all local government areas. Additionally, the soon-to-be-completed Dakkada Skills Acquisition Centre aims to empower students with the vocational and technical skills needed to thrive in today’s economy.

Free Education and Global Exchange Initiatives

Education in Akwa Ibom remains free and compulsory at the primary and secondary levels, with the state government covering WAEC and NABTEB exam fees. In a bid to make students globally competitive, the administration has integrated ICT and entrepreneurial training into the curriculum. A key highlight of the administration’s commitment to international exposure is the Educational Exchange Programme with the London Borough of Southwark, offering public school students the opportunity to learn in a new cultural environment.

Holistic Development and Student Welfare

The administration emphasizes the importance of holistic child development, urging parents and guardians to protect children from social vices. In alignment with this goal, Governor Eno approved the recruitment of 200 healthcare workers to strengthen primary healthcare services, ensuring students remain healthy and focused on their studies.

A Call to Build a Brighter Future Together

The governor’s passion for education stems from a belief that children are the future of Akwa Ibom. His message to students on Children’s Day underscores the administration’s commitment to nurturing young talents and creating opportunities for success. Through partnerships like those with the Akwa Ibom State Association in the U.S. (AKISAN), which supports education through book donations and STEM outreach programs, the government aims to foster continuous development.

Governor Eno’s educational reforms, rooted in the ARISE Agenda, are not just about infrastructure but about empowering the next generation to achieve their full potential. His call for stakeholders to join in these efforts reflects his administration’s drive to ensure sustainable growth and prosperity for all children in the state. With these initiatives, Akwa Ibom is setting the foundation for a brighter, more prosperous future, built on the pillars of education and skill development.

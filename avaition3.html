WHY WE ARE MAKING AKWA IBOM AVIATION HUB IN NIGERIA — GOV ENO

Governor Umo Eno of Akwa Ibom State has emphasized the significance of developing the aviation sector, stating that it is a key step toward positioning the state as the nation’s aviation hub.

Akwa Ibom is home to Ibom Air, a state-owned airline that operates both domestic and international flights. Recently, the airline expanded its fleet to seven aircraft, including five Bombardier CRJ 900 and two Airbus A220-300.

Speaking at the foundation-laying ceremony for the Aviation Village, a residential estate for airport workers within the Victor Attah International Airport, Governor Eno highlighted the project’s role in expanding the aviation ecosystem and reinforcing Akwa Ibom’s position as an aviation leader in Nigeria.

“The Aviation Village, for which we are performing the groundbreaking ceremony today, is a testament to our passionate commitment to growing the aviation sector,” the Governor stated.

“The first phase will provide housing for airport workers, further advancing our vision of becoming the nation’s major aviation hub. The second phase will include an aviation school and other facilities. Our research shows that 12 parastatals will benefit from this project, which will directly enhance airport operations.”

Governor Eno assured that the project, set for completion within 12 months, will be handled by multiple contractors to accelerate progress, starting with the perimeter fence and gatehouse. He also outlined recent achievements in the aviation sector, including the addition of four new aircraft to the Ibom Air fleet, further strengthening the airline's capacity and the state’s aviation infrastructure.

Looking ahead, Governor Eno expressed his vision of Akwa Ibom as a vibrant construction hub in 2025, with numerous projects planned under the state’s budget. He urged contractors to remain committed to delivering these projects efficiently.

Additionally, he announced plans to lay the foundation for an Elderly People’s Home and a new Medical Village for doctors at the Ibom Specialist Hospital. These initiatives aim to support the hospital’s collaboration with foreign technical partners and ensure it fulfills its original vision as a world-class medical facility.

A’ IBOM ICT & DIGITAL SERVICES DIRECTORATE MEETS TECH-COMMUNITY

The ICT and Digital Services Directorate of the Akwa Ibom State government has reaffirmed its commitment to collaborating with the state’s tech community in line with the Arise Agenda of Governor Umo Eno's administration to advance the ICT sector. This assurance was a central theme during the Directorate’s inaugural meeting with key players in the ICT sector, held at the Signature by Royalty Hotel in Ewet Housing Estate, Uyo.

The meeting brought together aides of the Governor from the Directorate, including the Senior Special Assistant to the Governor on ICT and Digital Services, Dr. Frank Ekpenyong; Personal Assistant to the Governor on IT Security, Network and Surveillance Systems, Mr. Andino Nkim; and Personal Assistant to the Governor on Data Warehousing and Artificial Intelligence, Mrs. Lucie Ekong. They engaged with members of the Akwa Ibom Tech Community, pledging to foster partnerships that drive innovation and develop impactful tech solutions for the benefit of the people.

Dr. Ekpenyong highlighted Governor Umo Eno’s visionary Arise Agenda, emphasizing the crucial role of the tech community in realizing the administration’s goals. He encouraged the tech leaders to align their expertise with the agenda and identify areas for collaboration that could translate the Governor’s vision into actionable outcomes. Mr. Nkim expressed gratitude to the tech community for embracing the Governor’s blueprint and demonstrating a willingness to collaborate. He urged them to remain steadfast in their pursuit of innovative initiatives, emphasizing their role as key partners in driving the state’s ICT growth.

The meeting featured participation from influential tech hub CEOs and members of various hubs, including Start Innovation Hub Uyo, The Roothub Uyo, LASkills Uyo, FutureLabs Ikot Ekpene, and Matrix Hub, among others.

The ICT and Digital Services Directorate, led by Dr. Frank Ekpenyong, also includes Engr. Solomon Eyo, Special Assistant to the Governor on Website and Multimedia Streaming; Mr. Andino Nkim, Personal Assistant to the Governor on IT Security, Network and Surveillance Systems; and Mrs. Lucie Ekong, Personal Assistant to the Governor on Data Warehousing and Artificial Intelligence. Together, they are driving the administration’s vision of leveraging technology to improve the lives of Akwa Ibom citizens.

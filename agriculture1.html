Governor Umo Eno's Visionary Ibom Model Farm: Pioneering Akwa Ibom's Agricultural Renaissance for Economic Growth and Food Security

In a progressive move to elevate agriculture in Akwa Ibom State, Governor Umo Eno has launched a series of transformative projects that underscore his commitment to making agriculture the foundation of the state’s economy. Through an ambitious and integrated agricultural approach, he has outlined a vision that promises food security, job creation, and long-term economic growth for Akwa Ibom.

One of the cornerstone projects in this initiative is the Ibom Model Farm, a 50,000-hectare agricultural complex located in Ikot Edibon, Nsit Ubium Local Government Area. Modeled after the Songhai Farm Technology in Porto-Novo, Benin Republic, this farm integrates various aspects of agricultural production—crop farming, animal husbandry, aquaculture, and agro-processing—to maximize productivity and economic output. The farm will also feature facilities like a juice processing plant, bakery, fish ponds, a feed mill, an abattoir, and even a tourism center with a hotel, conference hall, and zoo. This multifaceted approach reflects the governor’s goal to use agriculture as a powerful tool for diversifying the economy and positioning Akwa Ibom as a leading agribusiness hub in Nigeria.

At the core of Governor Eno’s agricultural agenda is the ARISE Agenda, which aligns with his campaign promise to reduce dependency on oil by leveraging agriculture. This agenda emphasizes food security, job creation, and economic resilience through innovative agricultural practices. By developing an agricultural hub with such an extensive range of facilities and resources, Governor Eno aims to stimulate growth across the agricultural value chain, creating roughly 1,000 direct jobs and many more through related businesses. Furthermore, the farm’s tourism and recreational facilities are expected to attract visitors and investors, further boosting the state’s economy.

The governor’s dedication to youth empowerment is evident through his initiative to train and mentor 31 young Akwa Ibomites in modern agricultural techniques. These young professionals, who received specialized training, are positioned to replicate the model farm’s success across other parts of the state. By equipping them with skills and providing mentorship, Governor Eno aims to establish a sustainable ecosystem where young people become future leaders and innovators in agriculture.

With a management structure that mirrors the efficiency and success of the state-owned Ibom Air, the Ibom Model Farm is poised for long-term success. Governor Eno’s approach ensures that the farm operates independently and professionally, fostering accountability and innovation. This professional framework promises to deliver high-quality agricultural products that meet international standards, thereby opening new markets and positioning Akwa Ibom as a competitive player on the global stage.

Governor Umo Eno’s agricultural transformation plan represents a bold shift towards a diversified economy. By implementing a cohesive and sustainable strategy, he is setting the stage for Akwa Ibom to emerge as a model of agricultural excellence in Nigeria and beyond, proving that with vision and commitment, agriculture can indeed be a powerful vehicle for socio-economic transformation.
